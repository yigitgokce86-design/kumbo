# Kumbo - Synthetic User Interview Guide

## Interview Objectives

1. **Validate Problem Hypotheses**: Confirm children struggle with abstract money concepts
2. **Explore Current Behaviors**: Understand how families currently manage allowance/saving
3. **Test Solution Concepts**: Gauge interest in goal-based saving with visual progress
4. **Identify Pain Points**: Discover frustrations with current methods
5. **Uncover Motivations**: Understand what drives both parents and children

---

## Interview Structure

### Part 1: Context & Background (5 min)
- Family composition and child ages
- Current financial practices with children
- Tech usage patterns

### Part 2: Problem Exploration (10 min)
- Current allowance/saving methods
- Pain points and frustrations
- Past attempts to teach financial concepts

### Part 3: Behavior Deep-Dive (10 min)
- Specific stories of saving/spending
- Goal-setting experiences
- Chore-reward connections

### Part 4: Solution Reaction (10 min)
- Concept presentation
- Feature prioritization
- Willingness to adopt

### Part 5: Wrap-Up (5 min)
- Ideal experience description
- Concerns and barriers
- Final thoughts

---

## Question Bank

### For Parents

#### Context
1. How many children do you have and what are their ages?
2. How would you describe your family's approach to teaching kids about money?
3. What apps or tools do your children currently use?

#### Problem Exploration
4. How do you currently handle allowance or pocket money?
5. What methods have you tried to teach your children about saving?
6. What's been most frustrating about teaching financial concepts to your kids?
7. Can you tell me about a time your child wanted something expensive? How did you handle it?

#### Behavior Deep-Dive
8. Do your children have savings goals? How do they track progress?
9. How do you connect chores or tasks to rewards?
10. What happens when your child receives money as a gift?
11. How does your child react when they have to wait for something they want?

#### Solution Reaction
12. If there was an app that let your child set a goal (like a toy) and earn toward it through chores, how would you react?
13. What features would be most important to you as a parent?
14. What would concern you about a financial app for your child?
15. How much would you be willing to pay for such a tool?

#### Wrap-Up
16. What would the ideal money-learning experience look like for your family?
17. What would stop you from trying a new app for this purpose?

---

### For Children (via parent description or adapted)

#### Understanding
1. Do you know what saving money means?
2. Is there something you really want right now? How are you getting it?

#### Experience
3. Do you have a piggy bank or somewhere you keep money?
4. Do you do chores at home? Do you get anything for them?
5. How do you feel when you have to wait for something you want?

#### Preferences
6. Would you like to see how close you are to getting something you want?
7. What would make saving money more fun?
8. Do you like games with progress bars and achievements?

## Interview Conduct Guidelines

1. **Empathy First**: Listen without judgment; validate feelings
2. **Stories Over Opinions**: Ask for specific examples, not generalizations
3. **Probe Deeper**: Follow "interesting" responses with "Tell me more"
4. **Avoid Leading**: Don't suggest answers; let insights emerge naturally
5. **Capture Quotes**: Record exact language for authentic insights
